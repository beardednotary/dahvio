FAQ

Frequently asked questions

What is the Air Force PFA?

The Air Force Physical Fitness Assessment (PFA) measures overall fitness for active duty and reserve members. It includes body composition, an aerobic event, push-ups, and a core strength component. All active duty Airmen and Space Force Guardians are required to pass annually.

How is the Air Force PT test scored?

The PFA produces a composite score out of 100 points. Aerobic fitness carries the highest weight, followed by push-ups and core. Scores are classified as Excellent (90+), Satisfactory (75–89), Marginal (60–74), or Unsatisfactory (below 60) per DAFMAN 36-2905.

What score do I need to pass the Air Force fitness test?

A composite score of 75 or higher earns a Satisfactory rating, which is the standard passing threshold. A score of 90 or above is rated Excellent. Scores between 60 and 74 are Marginal, and below 60 is Unsatisfactory per current DAFMAN 36-2905 standards.

What governs the Air Force fitness assessment?

The Air Force PFA is governed by DAFMAN 36-2905, the Department of the Air Force Manual that defines fitness assessment standards, component weights, scoring tables, and testing procedures for all USAF and USSF members.

Does this calculator work for Space Force (USSF)?

Yes. The app includes a branch toggle to switch between Air Force (USAF) and Space Force (USSF) standards. Both branches use DAFMAN 36-2905 as their governing fitness document.
