Why It Works

Built for the person who wants less friction, not more motivation theater.

The app design here is intentionally restrained. You open it, start a walk, and let the numbers stay in the background until you want them. Afterward, your session is logged and your streak moves forward if you showed up that day.

Fast start

Start walking without setup

One primary action gets you moving. No account creation, no menus to fight through, and no social layer before you can begin.

Live feedback

See the stats that matter

During a walk, Just Walk tracks elapsed time, step count, and estimated distance so you can check progress when you want it and ignore it when you do not.

Days you walked

Keep a clean visual history

The app turns completed walks into a minimal days-you-walked mosaic and a streak count that rewards consistency without overcomplicating the habit.

Privacy first

Your data stays on your phone

Walk sessions and preferences are stored locally on-device. DahVio Studios does not run analytics on your walk history and does not require a backend account.

How It Feels

A simple rhythm from first step to saved walk.

The product flow is intentionally small: start, walk, end, log. That makes it useful on the days when you do not want to think about a whole fitness system but still want the walk to count.

1

Start Walk

Tap the main button to begin a session. The app starts tracking time immediately and begins listening for motion and distance data locally on-device.

2

Walk

Keep the stats tucked away or tap to see them. Time, steps, and distance update while you move, with support for metric or imperial units.

3

Hold to End

Ending the session is intentional so you do not lose a walk to an accidental tap. When the walk ends, the session is logged to your device.

4

See the pattern

Your history turns into a streak and a simple visual map of the days you walked. You can also share that view as an image from the app.

What It Tracks

Just enough to make the habit visible.

Under the hood, the app keeps session summaries and a few preferences so it can restore your history and remember how you like the experience set up.

Walk sessions

Each saved session includes start and end time, total steps, and total distance so your walking history can be restored the next time you open the app.

Preferences

Just Walk remembers settings like theme mode, unit system, and small interface state so the app opens the way you left it.

Distance permissions

Distance estimation uses location access during an active walk. Raw route points are processed on-device and are not stored as a shareable map or uploaded to DahVio Studios.

Share and review hooks

The app can generate a share image for your days-you-walked mosaic and can ask iOS for a review prompt. Those flows are initiated from your device and do not create an account with us.

FAQ

Common questions

These are the practical questions users usually have before they install or while they are deciding whether the app fits their routine.

Does Just Walk need an account?

No. There is no sign-in flow, no user profile, and no server-side walk history required to use the app.

Does it work offline?

Yes. Walk history and preferences are stored on-device, so the core experience does not depend on a continuous connection.

Why would steps or distance show as unavailable?

That usually comes down to device support or permissions. Step count depends on motion data support, and distance depends on location services being enabled.

Can I clear my history later?

Yes. Because the data is local to your device, deleting the app removes locally stored walk history. If you add an in-app reset flow later, that page copy will still remain accurate.
