FAQ

Frequently asked questions

What is the Marine PFT?

The Marine Corps Physical Fitness Test (PFT) measures overall physical readiness through pull-ups or push-ups, core strength (crunches or plank), and a 3-mile run. It is a mandatory assessment for all active duty and reserve Marines.

What is a good Marine PFT score?

A score of 235 or higher is typically considered first class, though exact classifications depend on age and gender according to official MCO 6100.13A standards. A perfect score is 300 points.

How is the Marine PFT scored?

Each event is assigned a point value based on performance. The total score is the sum of all three events, with a maximum of 300 points—100 per event. Point values are determined by MCO 6100.13A scoring tables.

What order governs the Marine PFT?

The Marine PFT is governed by Marine Corps Order MCO 6100.13A, which outlines scoring standards, testing procedures, and requirements for all Marines.

Can I use pull-ups or push-ups for the PFT?

Yes. Marines can choose either pull-ups or push-ups for the upper body portion of the PFT. Point values differ between the two options per MCO 6100.13A guidelines. The calculator handles both.
